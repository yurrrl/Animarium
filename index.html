üìñ Animarium - Archive of the Soul

1. Definition and Acquisition

  • Definition: The Animarium is a personal book every sentient being has, symbolizing 'soul' (Anima) and 'archive' (-rium).
  • Essence: It records an individual's life and the narratives formed.

2. Rules of the Animarium

  • Story Slots: Limited to 5 permanent story slots.
  • Story Creation: Significant events or achievements can automatically become 'stories'.
  • Grade Assignment: Stories get a grade based on world impact.
    • Grading System: Folktale (ÎØºÎã¥) < Fairy Tale (ÎèôÌôî) < Tale (ÏÑ§Ìôî) < Epic (ÏÑúÏÇ¨) < Legend (Ï†ÑÏÑ§) < Myth (Ïã†Ìôî).
  • Story Enhancement:
    • Repeated similar experiences strengthen a story.
    • Enhancement can enrich content, deepen attributes, or raise the grade.
    • Enhancement conditions vary.
  • New Stories & Replacement:
    • If full, a 6th story requires a choice: keep 5 or replace one.
    • Unslotted stories are permanently erased.

3. Attribution of Story Attributes

  • Conceptual Attributes/Powers: Stories grant conceptual attributes (not direct magic/strength).
  • Scope of Influence: Affects self, presence, reputation, relationships, fate.
  • Attribute Changes:
    • Enhancement strengthens or deepens attributes.
    • Replacement removes old attributes and adds new ones.
  • Examples:
    • "Compassion..." (Fairy Tale) -> 'Compassion' attribute (reduces wariness).
    • "Endurance..." (Legend) -> 'Perseverance' attribute (enhances mental fortitude).
    • "Righteous person..." (Epic) -> 'Unyielding Will' & 'Weight of Truth' (conviction, persuasion).
    • "Sage..." (Myth) -> 'Insight of the Abyss' & 'Mark of Taboo' (insight, but distance/attention).
    • "First hero..." (Divine Myth) -> 'Symbol of Salvation' & 'Guide of Destiny' (hope symbol, world influence).

4. Story Resonance and Ego Manifestation

  • Objects sharing long or significant (Epic+) stories with an owner can develop a faint Ego as narrative energy imprints upon them.

‚òÄÔ∏è Lumina Empire

1. Empire's Name

  • Name: Lumina Empire. 'Lumina' signifies spreading 'light' (civilization/order) and represents glory and hegemonic power.

2. Social Atmosphere

  • Expansionist & Strength-Worshipping: Values ability over lineage, seeks expansion.
  • Order of Conviction: Justifies conquest with 'salvation,' 'civilization,' imposing its values, believing it's best for the world.

3. Main Species

  • Human Centrism: Humans are the core and ruling class, based on "The Aurean Guides" doctrine.

4. Values: The Aurean Guides

  • Name Meaning: 'Aurea' (radiant/supreme value) + 'Guides' (human mission to lead others).
  • Core Doctrines:
    1. Humans are Aurea's chosen guides, tasked with leading others.
    2. Aurea's light is order/power; strength establishes order and serves Aurea.
    3. Constant advance and expansion are devotion to Aurea.
    4. The Emperor ("Brightest Star") is sacred, an incarnation of Aurea, with absolute authority.

5. Social Structure and Governing Ideology

  • Structure: Medieval feudal society (Emperor, nobles, serfs).
  • Rule of "The Aurean Guides": Doctrine ingrained in laws, education, culture; a spiritual and governing pillar.
  • Meritocracy: Limited opportunities for status rise through ability/achievements, especially military.

6. Lumina Empire Major Power Systems

Knights (Í∏∞ÏÇ¨)

Knights possess superhuman combat abilities through 'Aura' training.

Aura (Ïò§Îùº)

  • Aura Core: Formed by condensing Mana in the heart, fused with vitality/mental strength; determines potential.
  • Bodily Circuits: Aura circulates through microscopic pathways, strengthening the body and increasing Aura capacity.
  • Aura Shaping: Precise control of Aura for enhancement, concentration, release, and shaping.

Knight Ranks

  • Aura User / Novice Knight: Basic body/weapon enhancement.
  • Aura Expert / Elite Knight: Precise control, significant enhancement, limited application techniques.
  • Aura Master / Grand Knight: Free manipulation, transcendent abilities, special techniques.
  • Armament: Heavy armor and destructive weapons enhanced by Aura.

Knight Organizations

  • Knight Orders under Lords: Local military foundation, loyal to their lords.
  • Luminous Knights: Emperor's elite order, superior in all aspects, guards Emperor and enforces his will.
Wizards (ÎßàÎ≤ïÏÇ¨)

Systematically trained mages manipulating 'Mana'.

Magic: Principle and System

  • Mana Sphere: Formed by condensing Mana in the heart, a reservoir for casting.
  • Spell Imprinting: Imprinting 'Spell Formulas' onto the Mana Sphere to define magic.
  • Floor Levels & Titles:
    • 1st~3rd Floor: Apprentice Mage (Basic learning).
    • 4th~6th Floor: Mage (Stable sphere, complex spells).
    • 7th Floor: Sorcerer (Unique level, original spells).
    • 8th~9th Floor: Archmage (Legendary, battlefield-changing power).

Wizard Organizations

  • Mage Tower-Centric System: Magic research/training centered in the capital's Mage Tower, under strict Imperial control.
  • Hierarchy and Management: Strict hierarchy based on floor/ability/contribution; elite but supervised group.

‚öôÔ∏è The Timocratic Republic - A Symphony of Wealth and Steam

1. Republic's Name

  • Name: The Arcadian Timocratic Republic (ÏïÑÎ•¥Ïπ¥ÎîîÏïà Ìã∞Î™®ÌÅ¨ÎùºÌã± Í≥µÌôîÍµ≠). Rose from the 'Great Revolution of Steel and Steam'. Wealth, not virtue, now dictates fate, justified by opposition to the Lumina Empire.

2. Social Atmosphere

  • Technological Worship & Polarization: Latest tech is praised, but benefits few, widening the wealth gap.
  • Public Unity & Private Grievances: Official unity against Lumina, but private dissatisfaction with inequality exists.
  • Anxious Life Under Surveillance: Overt surveillance for 'national security' stifles free expression.
  • Fierce Struggle for Survival: Intense competition for wealth and opportunity.

3. Main Species

  • Pherians:
    • Diverse animalistic traits (wolfkin, catkin, etc.) with varied manifestation.
    • Superior physical abilities and instincts.
    • Distinct sensory organs.
  • Chthonians:
    • Short (1.1~1.4m), sturdy, muscular build.
    • Excellent dexterity, concentration, suited for machinery/mining.
    • Strong endurance, adapted to underground/harsh work.
    • Males often have stiff, full beards.

4. Republic's Values

  • Plutocracy: Wealth is the highest value; ability judged by possessions.
  • Illusion of Progress: Tech development symbolizes power; belief it can solve all problems.
  • Extreme Efficiency & Pragmatism: Everything judged by efficiency; non-practical arts/philosophy disregarded unless profitable.

5. Social Structure and Governing Ideology

  • Structure: Officially a republic, but voting rights tied to wealth, creating a divide between the enfranchised, laborers, and marginalized.
  • "Timocracy" Rule: 'Wealth equals ability' principle ingrained in laws, education, politics.
  • Performance-Based Opportunity: Limited opportunities for status rise via wealth/tech, but biased towards those with existing capital.

Methodos Ranks

  • Gamma Grade: Basic training, standardized portfolio, support roles.
  • Beta Grade: Advanced training, specialized stories, complex missions.
  • Alpha Grade: Highest level, exceptional stories, national crisis/top-secret ops.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Utlander - Free Savages Beyond the Border

A collective term for various tribes in "Utland" (Outer Land), north of Lumina. Mostly 'Halbriso' (giant-blooded), plus humans and others. Seen as uncivilized.

1. Living Environment (Utland)

  • Harsh Climate: Long cold winters, short cool summers; snow, glaciers, fjords, taiga, tundra.
  • Natural Threats: Blizzards, rugged terrain, large snow wolves, giant bears, mammoths.

2. Main Species

  • Halbriso:
    • Overwhelming height (Males 2.5-3m, Females 2.3-2.7m).
    • Immense strength, tough skin/bones.
    • Rapid growth, short lifespan (unstable giant blood).
    • Coarse, intimidating appearance.
    • Adapted via physical strength/experience.

3. Lifestyle and Social Structure

  • Respect for Choice: Minimal rules, values individual freedom/responsibility; can leave tribes.
  • Diverse Lifestyles: Settled tribes (herding/villages), nomadic tribes, mountain tribes, sea-faring/raiding tribes.
  • Raiding Culture: Means for survival, display of strength, gaining honor.
  • Jovial Festival Culture: Boisterous feasts, songs, competitions after hunts/battles.

4. Values

  • Honor and Freedom: Highest values; detest coercion.
  • Exploration and Adventure: Curiosity, seeking new experiences/challenges/honor.
  • Flexibility: Respect customs but will change them if they hinder survival/development.

üèúÔ∏è Al-Azuma - Tomb of Forgotten Greatness

Vast desert beyond the Republic, holding colossal ruins of a hyper-ancient civilization. Inhabited by Azmari. Some Republic tech originated here; generally seen as a dangerous, secret-filled expanse.

1. Living Environment

  • Extreme Climate: Scorching days, freezing nights. Powerful sandstorms (Samum). Scarce water (oases, ruins).
  • Desolate Terrain: Red sand dunes, badlands, mesas, buttes, canyons, intertwined with massive ancient ruins.

2. Primary Species

  • Azmari:
    • Taller, slender, graceful yet hardy physique.
    • Desert adapted (skin, eyes, temperature resistance).
    • Long lifespan, ancient traces (skin patterns, hair color).
    • Long, pointed ears for sensitive hearing.
    • Technologically adept dexterity.

3. Lifestyle and Social Structure

  • Solitary & Sparse: Many wanderers; some settlements near oases/ruins.
  • Lawless & Pragmatic: No formal laws; survival dictates actions, disputes settled by strength/negotiation.
  • Resource Tension: Fierce competition for water, food, ancient artifacts.
  • Distrustful & Insular: Deep distrust of outsiders; occasional secret trade.
  • Transport/Equipment: Mechanical contraptions from ruins; desert robes augmented with artifacts.

4. Values

  • Survivalism: Survival is the ultimate priority.
  • Freedom & Independence: Detest external control.
  • Ambivalence to Knowledge: Ruins offer advantages but are viewed with suspicion; some lore is taboo.
  • Practicality: Tangible results valued over words.
